Aurora: Design Considerations for High Throughput Cloud Native Relational Databases (SIGMOD 2017)

May 2026 paper reading

Problem

Opening paragraph. Set up the paper or topic — what problem does it address and why does it matter. Keep this punchy, one or two sentences.

Background

Context the reader needs before you get into the paper itself. What was the state of the art before this work? What pain point motivated it?

The core idea

What is the paper actually proposing? Explain it in your own words, not the abstract's words. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet.

What I found interesting

The most valuable part of your writeup. What surprised you? What's more subtle than it first appears? What design decision is load-bearing?

What I found unconvincing

Where does the paper's argument weaken? What does the evaluation not test? What assumptions might not hold in practice?

How it connects to other work

How does this paper relate to others you've read? What does it respond to, build on, or contradict? This is where your longitudinal reading pays off.

Takeaway

One or two sentences. What should the reader walk away remembering?
